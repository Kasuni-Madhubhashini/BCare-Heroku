What is a Breast Cancer?

Breast cancer is a disease in which the cells of the breast grow out of control. There are different types of breast cancer. The type of breast cancer depends on which cells in the breast become cancerous. Breast cancer can start in different parts of the breast. A breast is made up of three main parts: lobules, ducts, and connective tissue. Lobules are the glands that make milk. The ducts are tubes that carry milk to the nipple. Connective tissue (consisting of fibrous and fatty tissue) surrounds and holds everything together. Most breast cancers start in the ducts or lobules. Breast cancer can spread outside of the breast through blood vessels and lymphatic vessels. If breast cancer spreads to other parts of the body, it is said to be metastatic.

Types of Breast Cancer

1.Benign Breast Cancer (Non-Invasive)

That kind of cancer does not spread to neighboring tissue regions and therefore it is rarely life threatening. These cells remain completely at their place of origin because they haven't already developed the ability to spread out of these ducts, either within the breast or in another part of the body. The Cancer cells most often develop in milk ducts and therefore it is also known as ductal carcinoma Cancer in situ (DCIS). Both men and women can Develop DCIS.

2. Malignant Breast Cancer (Invasive)

Malignant or Invasive is the type of cancer which have Possibility of spreading from the breast to other parts of the body and life threatening. It can often be removed but sometimes it grows back. The most common type of Invasive breast cancer is invasive ductal cancer. These accounts for 80% of all breast cancer case

3. Other types of Breast Cancer

The least common type of breast cancer includes the invasive lobular breast cancer that develops in the cells of the milk-producing lobules, inflammatory breast cancer, tubular breast cancer, medullary and papillary breast cancer

Symptoms of Breast Cancer

• New lump in the breast or underarm (armpit).
• Any change in the size or the shape of the breast.
• Thickening or swelling of part of the breast.
• Irritation or dimpling of breast skin. (like orange peel)
• Redness or flaky skin in the nipple area or the breast.
• Pulling in of the nipple or pain in the nipple area and Nipple discharge other than breast milk, including blood.
